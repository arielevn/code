Elon Reeve Musk

Elon Musk is a South African-born American entrepreneur and businessman who founded X.com in 1999 (which later became PayPal), SpaceX in 2002 and Tesla Motors in 2003. Musk became a multimillionaire in his late 20s when he sold his start-up company, Zip2, to a division of Compaq Computers.

Here's the timeline of Elon Musk

  • 1971: Elon Musk is born on June 28th in Pretoria, South Africa.
  • 1989-1995: Musk attends the University of Pretoria and later transfers to the University of Pennsylvania in the United States.
  • 1995: Musk drops out of Stanford University after just two days to pursue entrepreneurial ventures
  • 1995-2002: Co-founds Zip2, a company that provides online business directories and maps for newspapers. Compaq acquires Zip2 in 1999 for around $307 million.
  • 1999: Musk co-founds X.com, an online payment company. X.com later becomes PayPal through a series of mergers and acquisitions.
  • 2002: PayPal is acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion, and Musk earns a substantial payout from the sale.
  • 2002: Musk founds SpaceX (Space Exploration Technologies Corp.) with the goal of reducing space transportation costs and enabling the colonization of Mars.
  • 2004: SpaceX's first privately developed rocket, Falcon 1, makes its inaugural flight.
  • 2004: Musk invests in Tesla Motors (now Tesla, Inc.), an electric car company that was founded in 2003. He becomes the company's chairman of the board.
  • 2006: Tesla unveils its first electric car, the Tesla Roadster.
  • 2008: SpaceX's Falcon 1 becomes the first privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit.
  • 2008: SpaceX wins a $1.6 billion contract from NASA to develop the Falcon 9 rocket and Dragon spacecraft for cargo transport to the International Space Station (ISS).
  • 2010: Tesla becomes a publicly traded company with an initial public offering (IPO) on the NASDAQ stock exchange.
  • 2012: SpaceX's Dragon spacecraft successfully docks with the ISS, becoming the first commercial spacecraft to do so.
  • 2013: Musk reveals the concept of the Hyperloop, a high-speed transportation system using pods in near-vacuum tubes.
  • 2015: Tesla introduces the Model X, an all-electric SUV.
  • 2015: SpaceX succeeds in landing the first stage of its Falcon 9 rocket back on Earth, a significant step towards reusability in spaceflight.
  • 2016: Tesla announces its Model 3, a more affordable electric car aimed at a broader market.
  • 2017: SpaceX unveils plans for the Interplanetary Transport System (later renamed Starship), a spacecraft intended for interplanetary travel, including potential Mars colonization.
  • 2018: SpaceX launches the Falcon Heavy, the most powerful rocket in operation at the time, with Musk's personal Tesla Roadster as the payload.
  • 2020: SpaceX's Crew Dragon spacecraft successfully carries astronauts to the ISS, marking a significant milestone in commercial crewed spaceflight.
  • 2020: Musk becomes the world's richest person for a brief period, largely due to the increase in Tesla's stock price.
